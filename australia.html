Visit Sydney

Live amongst the playful city and suburbs of Sydney, Australia! Travel among the waterside and stand in awe of the sail-like shape of the Sydney Opera House. Visit the Royal Botanic Garden and make furry friends at the Taronga Zoo. Home to Bondi Beach and a spectacular wine region, there is something for everyone. Take weekend trips to the bush to become one with nature, or simply bask in the beauty of the University of Sydney!

Curriculum

ARTH214: Investigate indigenous Australian art, including that made by the Aboriginal Australian and Torres Strait Islander people. Explore all types of art media, from leaf painting, rock carving, cermonial clothing, and sand painting. 3 credits.

ART112: A hands-on 3D art class centered on Indigenous textiles and jewelry. Projects include basket weaving and kalti paarti, a traditional artform made by carving emu eggs. 3 credits.

REL141: The symbols and spiritual beliefs of Aboriginal people. Research rituals and how religion shaped clans' social structure. Read and discuss transcribed oral stories of ancestral spirits, creators of the land and sky. Compare to other widely-known mythologies. 3 credits.

BIO379: Draw upon real-world biological problems and data sets to reach scientific conclusions. Lectures cover statistical modeling and develop students' knowledge of experimentation design. Lab time gives students hands-on opportunities to enhance their understanding of biology. Prerequisite: BIO279. 4 credits.

GEOL112: Investigate Australia's geological time periods over the last 3.8 billion years. Study the movements of the Indo-Australian plate. Extrapolate data from studies of plate separation to predict future trends of movement. Many classes are held outdoors. 3 credits.

Language Requirements

All courses are catered towards English-speaking students.
