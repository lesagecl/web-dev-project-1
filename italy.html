Visit Rome

Immerse yourself in the rich history and culture of the "Eternal City," Rome! Explore ancient monuments and the remains of a glorious civilization. All students will explore the Roman Baths, St. Peter's Square, and the Trevi Fountain during their visit. For an additional fee, a weekend trip to the island of Burano in Venice is available. All courses are held in partnership with John Cabot University.

Curriculum

REL141: A religion class concentrated in the beliefs of acient civilizations. Study the practice of polytheism and how religion spreads through oral tradition. Includes a field trip to the Pantheon. 3 credits.

ITAL101: The foundations of the Italian language. Learn elementary Italian through reading, writing, listening, and speaking. Develop language comprehension skills. Required unless the student has already completed the course through CJU. 3 credits.

HIST133: Dive into the history of ancient Roman civilization and city-states from 8th century BC to 5th century BC. Develop a deep understanding of military structure and its cultural impact. 3 credits.

HUM204: Learn Italian culture through the study of architecture, painting, and sculpture. Dissect ancient literature in student seminars. On the last week, class is held in the university kitchen to prepare and appreciate Italian cuisine. 3 credits.

POSC157: Explore how the Roman political system has influenced our government today. Develops a foundation for understanding how government is built around a ruling elite. 3 credits.

Language Requirements

Students are not required to have any prior knowledge of the Italian language. All courses are catered towards English-speaking students.
