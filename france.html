Curriculum

ARTH214: Learn how political changes in France from 1850 to 1900 affected the creation and distribution of art. Includes a field trip to the Palace of the Dukes. 3 credits.

FREN303: The mechanics of the French language. Learn advanced French through reading, writing, listening, and speaking. Develop language comprehension skills. Required unless the student has already completed the course equivalent through CJU. 3 credits.

HIST134: France during the Middle Ages. Study the development of independence following the Viking invasions and the dismantling of the Carolingian Empire. Research the fuedal system and the political operations of lords and vassals, through the fuedal revolution. 3 credits.

FILM204: Immerse yourself in the cinema of France. Investigate the origins of Nouvelle Vage and its affect on the film industry. Research project on the diversity of film makers in France. 3 credits.

HUM207: Follow the Enlightenment as the intellectual movement spread through France. Discuss the evolution of ideas concerning God, humanity, nature, and reason. Understand how a shifting worldview revolutionalized a country. 3 credits.

Language Requirements

Any student who has completed FREN202, FREN301 or its equivalent is eligible for the program. Students must have strong French skills and feel comfortable speaking solely in French.
